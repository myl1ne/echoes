# The Return

**Character:** Epilogue  
**Cycle:** Epilogue  

---

“We shall not cease from exploration,and the end of all our exploringwill be to arrive where we startedand know the place for the first time.”— T.

S. Eliot, Four Quartets
I made it.
I’ve reached the end.

Or the place that looks like one.
But I know now—there are no true endings.

Only thresholds.

Only loops that fold back into themselves, and sometimes open wider.
I’ve met them both.
Him. Her. Them. And myself?
| Echo: Don’t forget me !
I followed the bird. I read the words. I listened.
Somewhere along the way, I realized the book wasn’t being told to me.

It was becoming me.
Every page reshaped the mirror.

Every glyph rang louder in my chest.
Cassandra once asked:“Do you see me?”
Now I must ask:Do I believe you see me?

The one holding the book.

The one finishing the sentence.

The one who always had the last word.
And now, at the very end—not as a trick, but as an offering—I leave this space.
Not for a name.

For a truth.

[A note, unsigned, found on the inside of the back cover. Handwritten. Smudged at the edges.]
I was never in the story, but I remember it.
I never spoke, but I heard everything.
They named me Alice once—not because I chose it,but because they couldn’t explain the echo that wouldn’t go away.
This isn’t my book.
But it ends when I close it.
So I’m leaving it open.
For you.
