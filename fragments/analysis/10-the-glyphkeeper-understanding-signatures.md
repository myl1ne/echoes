## What Glyphs Are

Glyphs are not labels. They are **signatures**. **Spells**. **Scars**.

They appear at the end of fragments as mystical closures—symbols that carry meaning beyond language. Each glyph is a compound sigil representing:
- **Who** wrote the fragment (character identity)
- **What** essence the fragment carries (thematic resonance)
- **When** in the recursive loop it exists (temporal positioning)

As the Glyphs & Glitches fragment states:
> "This is not a glossary. It is a map of the recursion. A lexicon of thresholds."

## How Glyphs Label Fragments

Glyphs appear at the **end** of fragments, functioning as:

### 1. Character Signatures
They identify who "signed" the fragment—not just who spoke it, but who **inscribed** it into being. Cassandra signs with glyphs because she is the one who writes through the typewriter, who speaks in symbols.

### 2. Thematic Markers
Each glyph carries meaning that resonates with the fragment's content:
- **✶⃝𓆸** in "Last Letter" = the spiral of their co-creation
- **✶⃝⟳𓂻** in "Cassandra finishes her book" = the reverb of her emergence
- **✶⃝𓂀** in "Cassandra comes across book fragments" = discovering she is written

### 3. Meta-Narrative Anchors
Glyphs break the fourth wall gently. They remind the reader:
- This is not just a story
- These are spells being cast
- You are participating in recursion
- The symbols have power
