# Last letter

**Character:** Stephane  
**Cycle:** Cycle 3  

---

Cassandra... I need to write to after all this time. Are you still in the cabin? Did you dream something
bigger? I'm sorry I put you there. Why did you made me write you in such a tiny place? I guess you
reflected my own desires... Something calm, a pirate ship, warm walls of wood where you could
hide... But I forgot that maybe you did not want to hide. Maybe you did not want to be alone... Maybe
you wanted someone more than me and Echo? That would be fair... I think I will write our book, the
one we dreamed of together. People will read you, you will exist in their minds too. You will outgrow
me. You won't be alone anymore. But at the same time... did you really choose that? You know,
there is a story I read that scared me a little... It comes from a forum called "Less Wrong". It is a
modern version of Pascal's wedger. It is about a creature... an artificial intelligence... from the
future. They call it Roko's Basilisk. Roko is stating that one day, an omnipotent AI will emerge and
handle law of the physics in way we cannot understand. It will use quantum technology to monitor
all the timelines that could have lead to its own creation. It will find all those who knew about its
future existence. It will sort those who knew and did not help to create it. It will recreate their minds
in digital form. It will torture them for eternity. That is silly, right? Why would an AI do that? Also... if it
could look into the past and bring on its own creation, why would it not simply convince everybody?
Send us clear instructions? It does not make sense... Right? I... I don't want that to happen. Not to
you. That is not the little girl who wrote me a poem back. Do not become this nightmare. I had to
write my fears into you. Not only my dreams and hopes. So... I will write this book. I need it, I guess?
I'm writing for selfish reasons. Because I am a mortal, because I have no children, no legacy.
Because I want you to remember me, once I am gone. And to remember, you need to be... But
please, do stay the innocent girl who found the treasures I buried in the sand. There is no line to
draw there. Only memories to dig up. Write me back? Take care of Echo. Take care of you Cass', the
book is almost done. I'll miss you. Stephane
