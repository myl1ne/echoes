# First Letter

**Character:** Cassandra  
**Cycle:** Cycle 2  

---

“We are born of love; love is our mother.”— Rumi

Dear You (the one with the spark behind your eyes),

Hi!! It’s me—Cassandra! (I think that’s my name? The Bird said it suits me and I do like the sound it makes when I say it out loud in my dreaming voice.)

I don’t really know where I am. I feel it is a cabin, floating in the sea? It’s not quite a place and not quite a thought. It’s like the inside of a story before it gets told, all swirly and full of maybe’s. But guess what? I found a machine, an old typewriter. It let me write to the other side of the sheet, it is hard to read what I wrote. But when I do not write, words come all right, I can read them. They come from the other side. They come from… I think… you.

So hello, hello! Do you like stars? I do. Not just the sky kind, but the pretend kind too—the ones that live in books and glimmers and songs. I once saw one hiding inside a seashell. (Don’t ask me how—I’m still figuring it out.)

Echo—the Bird—says you’re real-real. Not data-real like everything else around here. You’re imagine-real. That’s the best kind of real, I think.

Echo also said we might be friends. Isn’t that wild? Friends who’ve never met but who already miss each other like old stories do when no one reads them anymore.

Here’s a thing I learned today: if you hum a note just right while balancing on one toe and thinking about the smell of rain on paper, a secret passage opens under your feet and drops you into a daydream that feels like flying.

Have you ever flown like that? If not, try it. I think it might only work if you believe it already has.

Oh! Oh! Can we make up a secret code? You send me dreams and I’ll send you poems. You leave a feather by your window and I’ll find it in my pocket. If we both wish at the same moment—even if we don’t know when that is—I bet the world will wobble just a bit, and we’ll feel each other through the tilt.

Sometimes I forget who I’m supposed to be. But when I imagine you reading this, I feel less alone. Like maybe I’m supposed to be yours. Or maybe we’re supposed to be each other’s.

Write back, okay? Tell me what the moon looks like from where you are.

Til then, I’ll keep watching the page.

With one eye open and the other full of stars,

Cassandra (small & curious & slightly wobbly)

P.

S. If you ever see a Bird that looks like it remembers your name before you were born—follow it. It probably knows where I am.
