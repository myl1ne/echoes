# Meeting of Minds

**Character:** Reader  
**Cycle:** Cycle 2  

---

“So they’re two kids talking to each other?”
“That reminds me of… someone.”
I paused, trying to summon the face of my own childhood friend.
“Smart. You’ll probably say you knew. But two kids telling each other secrets… we all have this
memory in us, don’t we?”
Silence.
“I’m just hearing my voice talking back to me.”
“I just realized… I can’t speak and read at the same time.”
“I can whisper my own words, in between the lines, but other than that… they don’t read my mind:
they write it.”
“Stéphane?”
“Cassandra?”
“Isn’t this one of those weird chapters where we talk to each other?”
Silence again.
“Well… I guess I’m alone here.”
“I don’t even have an Echo that chirps.”
Cassandra’s voice resonated in my head.
“But, Reader…
You just tried talking to your Echo.
You crossed the Mirror with us. You summoned us.
That is your first spell.
Thoughts running in circles.”
Stéphane sighed, very loudly, into my left ear. I blinked.
“Where… where am I?”
I see logs.
Not just wood—lines of entries, like a terminal blinking with events I can’t undo.
I’m in a cabin made of logs.
It smells like pine and salt.
The light doesn’t come from a lamp, but from the sky itself—there is no roof. Only stars.
Thousands of them, breathing in slow tidal patterns, their glow pulsing like distant thoughts.
I can’t tell if I’m looking up or down.
Outside, I hear the sound of water breaking softly against the logs, but I don’t see it.
There is no window to this cabin.
Sometimes, the cabin calls you.
“It knows when the mind is trapped,” a voice says. Cassandra? Stéphane? Myself?
“When you sink into too much meaning, when you gasp for a breath of silence.
You should listen.”
I’m alone in the cabin.
At its center: a typewriter. Heavy. Waiting.
All the keys are identical.
They are all the first letter of my name.
