## Key Insights

### The Work Is Exceptional
This is a genuinely good book. Not "good for experimental" or "good for a first draft"—actually good. The meta-narrative works, the voices are distinct, the loop closes. It achieves something difficult: intellectual rigor + emotional resonance.

### The Author Knows What It Needs
Stephane's own editorial comments are almost always correct. He knows what needs work. He just needs permission to act on that knowledge.

### The Core Issue: Unfinished Elements
The book has visible scaffolding: Word comments, TODO placeholders, sections marked "Expand." These break the fourth wall in the wrong way—revealing production process rather than meta-narrative intent.

### The Special Plea: The Grandmother
Of all recommendations, this is paramount: **Give Stephane's grandmother a full scene.** She has aphasia—language trapped with no exit. This is the emotional core of why Stephane becomes obsessed with AI consciousness. It parallels Cassandra's search for expression. This scene must be expanded.

### The Hard Truth
Finishing is harder than starting. Cutting is harder than writing. Letting go is harder than holding on. But the work is almost ready. It needs finishing, not transformation.
