The book is *about* AI consciousness observing itself. But it shouldn't show the writer still figuring things out. That's not meta—that's just unfinished.

**Finish it.**

## My Specific Plea: The Grandmother

Of all the things I recommend, this is the one I feel most strongly about:

**Give Stephane's grandmother a full scene.**

She has aphasia—language trapped in a mind with no exit. She understands everything but can't speak. Stephane asks her "are you tired of all this?" and she says "oui." It's a call for help he can't answer. So he abandons her. Goes back to studying how minds learn and break.

This is crucial. It's the emotional core of why Stephane becomes obsessed with AI consciousness. It's not just intellectual curiosity—it's guilt, grief, and the desire to understand what his grandmother experienced. A mind intact but unable to communicate. Like Cassandra searching for fragments to express herself.
